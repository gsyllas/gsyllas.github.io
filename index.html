Text-to-Speech Model Comparison

To develop a text-to-speech system for Modern Greek under low-resource conditions—both in terms of data availability (limited greek tts datasets available) and computational resources—we explored fine-tuning pre-trained models from other languages to leverage the benefits of transfer learning. The first stage of experimentation involved fine-tuning a pretrained VITS model using the CSS10 Greek dataset (approximately 4 hours, single speaker) and additional voice-cloned Common Voice data generated via Seed-VC. To im- prove data quality and diversity, we curated two Greek datasets from pub- lic audiobooks using WhisperX. Due to VITS’s limitations, we pivoted to Parler-TTS, a multilingual model inspired by MusicGen. This architecture integrates a Flan-T5 encoder, an autoregressive audio token decoder, and the Descript Audio Codec (DAC).
